Universal Commerce Protocol overview

How a chat request becomes a checkout

A buyer does not move once through a funnel. They ask, compare, refine, add to cart, and loop through tool calls until the checkout path is clear.

AI Agent conversation
Each turn can trigger another search, detail lookup, cart update, or checkout action.
1

Buyer asks the AI

Intent, budget, style, destination, and image signals enter the conversation.

2

Agent calls tools

The MCP connector turns conversation context into UCP-ready requests.

3

Catalog finds matches

Products, images, prices, variants, and merchant signals come back as data.

4

Cart or checkout updates

The agent prepares line items, buyer context, totals, and the next status.

5

Buyer reviews or refines

The buyer can approve, ask for changes, or return to step 1 with better context.

5 can return to 1 many times before the buyer is ready to finish.
Catalog answers "what should I buy?"

The agent can search across Shopify-powered merchants, narrow by destination, and show product details without sending the buyer across many storefronts.

Checkout answers "where do I safely pay?"

The merchant keeps checkout rules and payment trust. The agent helps prepare the session, then hands off when buyer review or payment is needed.

Takeaway: UCP turns shopping chat into an iterative commerce loop while merchants keep product truth, checkout policy, and payment trust.

Web search vs UCP search

Why Catalog changes how AI finds products

A buyer using an AI agent enters a different decision space. The agent can either fall back to the SEO-driven web, or call UCP surfaces that expose product truth and buying actions.

Outside the agent

Browser search world

SEO, ads, and crawled pages still influence what gets exposed before the buyer enters an AI-led shopping conversation.

SEO ads crawled pages exposure spend

AI Agent conversation
Ad influence is weaker inside the recommendation loop; tool-call quality matters more.
Without UCP

Agent searches the web

Discovery depends on a search index and page parsing.

1
Product facts can be stale or hidden in human-facing pages.
2

With UCP

Agent calls commerce

Catalog, cart, and checkout data become readable to the agent.

1
Products, variants, images, inventory, delivery fit, and prices are structured.
2
Cart additions and checkout handoff become supported next actions.
Later in the journey

Conversion becomes the battleground

More add-to-cart moments

The agent can move selected variants closer to purchase when the data is actionable.

More checkout investment

Merchants compete on confidence, fulfillment, payment trust, and handoff quality.

Old focus: pay for exposure Large brands could dominate discovery by spending heavily on reach, ads, and search visibility.
→
New focus: earn the agent's choice Structured data and checkout readiness help the agent recommend, add to cart, and guide the buyer to completion.
Takeaway: The competition shifts from being found by humans to being chosen by AI, then converted through a trusted checkout path.
